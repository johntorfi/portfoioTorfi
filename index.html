Machine Learning application

In this project, a predictive model was developed to estimate insurance premiums based on a Kaggle dataset. The dataset included factors such as age, sex, BMI, number of children, smoking status, region, and insurance expenses. A comprehensive analysis was conducted, including data exploratory analysis, preprocessing, and evaluation of different machine learning algorithms. The dataset was divided into training and testing sets, and three algorithms (Linear Regression, Decision Tree, and Random Forest) were employed for prediction. Evaluation metrics such as R^2 score were utilized to assess the performance of each algorithm. The results demonstrated that the Random Forest model achieved the highest predictive accuracy with an R^2 score of 0.896. Additionally, a Flask web application was created and deployed to provide users with an interactive platform for estimating insurance premiums based on their personal details. This project highlights the potential of machine learning techniques in insurance premium estimation and suggests avenues for future improvement, such as feature engineering and exploring additional datasets.

Analysis of Customer Churn in Telecommunication Industry Using Machine Learning

In this study focused on the telecommunication industry, customer churn analysis was conducted using decision trees and random forests algorithms. The analysis provided insights into the factors contributing to customer churn, such as pricing, contract terms, and service features. Higher monthly charges, shorter tenure, and lower total charges were found to be correlated with increased churn rates. The decision tree and random forests algorithms demonstrated their predictive capabilities, achieving an accuracy of 93.17% on the test dataset. These findings enable businesses to devise targeted strategies to improve customer retention and loyalty, ultimately reducing churn rates and enhancing customer relationship management in the telecommunication industry.
